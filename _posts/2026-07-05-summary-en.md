## [Competence Gate: Gating Tool-Use via Internal Confidence](https://www.reddit.com/r/MachineLearning/comments/1unw5un/competence_gate_gating_tooluse_on_a_small_models/) ⭐️ 8.0/10

A 10MB LoRA adapter for Qwen3.5-4B that gates tool use based on internal activation confidence rather than verbalized confidence has been released, improving error detection and reducing private data leakage. The adapter decides per query whether to answer directly, search the web, or retrieve from local documents. Small language models are often overconfident and cannot accurately verbalize their uncertainty; this approach uses internal signals to gate tool use, making small models more reliable. It could enable safer deployment in privacy-sensitive or high-stakes applications by reducing both hallucinations and inadvertent data leaks. The gate achieved a d′ improvement of 0.46 (95% CI [0.01, 0.89]) in catching its own errors, and 87% of cases flagged by the gate that the base model missed were genuinely wrong. A two-signal version cut the rate of private questions sent to public search from 22% to 10% (reduction 0.12, 95% CI [0.02, 0.22]).

reddit · r/MachineLearning · /u/Synthium- · Jul 5, 07:49

**Background**: LoRA (Low-Rank Adaptation) is a parameter-efficient fine-tuning method that freezes pre-trained weights and injects trainable matrices, enabling lightweight adaptation. d′ (d-prime) is a measure of signal detection sensitivity that accounts for response bias. GGUF is a binary file format optimized for fast loading and inference of models, commonly used with llama.cpp and Ollama.

## [F-Droid Labels Google's ADV as Malware on 4 Billion Devices](https://f-droid.org/2026/07/01/adv-malware.html) ⭐️ 8.0/10

F-Droid, a free and open source Android app repository, has declared Google's Android Developer Verifier (ADV) a malware that will block apps not approved by Google, with activation beginning in Brazil, Indonesia, Singapore, and Thailand on September 30, 2026, and global rollout by 2027. This claim highlights a potential threat to Android's openness and user choice, as ADV could allow Google to block alternative app stores like F-Droid and restrict software freedom. It could affect billions of users and app developers who rely on side-loading. F-Droid states that ADV runs as a system service with root privileges on Android 8 and higher, cannot be removed, and is pre-installed on approximately 4 billion devices. Google allegedly avoids defining malware in its terms of service, allowing it to arbitrarily classify unwanted software like ad blockers as malware.

F-Droid is a repository for free and open source Android apps, often used as an alternative to Google Play Store. The controversy stems from ADV's ability to block apps not approved by Google, which critics argue reduces user control and software freedom.
